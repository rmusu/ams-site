The problem

Event accommodation breaks the tools it's managed with.

A major event means hundreds of properties, thousands of rooms, dozens of client organisations — each with contracts, entitlements, phased release rights and rooming lists that change daily. Generic hotel software counts total rooms; events live and die night by night. The result, almost everywhere: allocation done by hand in Excel, weeks of work, and numbers nobody can fully reconstruct.

Inventory that loses nights

A booking system that tracks "80 rooms" can't tell you that on 12 February you're 6 rooms short. AMS stores every room per hotel × type × night — availability is always the worst night of the stay, never an average.

Manual allocation rounds

Matching hundreds of requests to properties by hand takes weeks and satisfies no one. The AMS engine proposes a full round in seconds — priorities, entitlements and category blocks respected — and your team stays in control of every line.

Numbers nobody can defend

Who released those 40 rooms? When, and at what penalty? Every movement in AMS is an append-only ledger entry with a named user. Counters are reproducible from history — auditable by construction.

The foundation

A room-night ledger, not a room counter.

  • Contracts, allocations, releases and stop-sells recorded per night, per room type, per hotel.
  • Shared physical pools: sell a Double as single-use at its own rate, from the same rooms.
  • Nightly rates by date range — shoulder nights priced differently from event nights.
  • Every counter rebuildable from the movement history, with one click.
One system, three portals

Everyone works on the same numbers.

No exports, no reconciliation meetings: operations, client organisations and hotels each get their own portal on a single live inventory.

OPERATIONS

Back-office

Dashboard, per-night inventory grid, client files, allocation studio, release phases, purchase orders, full audit. Role-based access with optional MFA.

CLIENTS

Client portal

NOCs, federations, media and partners submit ranked requests with per-night demand, review proposals, sign the AAA in-platform, release rooms within their allowance and load rooming lists.

HOTELS

Supplier portal

Each property sees its confirmed allocation live, keeps its factsheet current, registers pro-forma invoices against purchase orders and downloads its allocation report.

Allocation Studio

Weeks of Excel become an afternoon of review.

  • Serve organisations by your ranking — never first-come-first-served.
  • Entitlements cap what each client can draw; pre-allocation blocks protect each group's rooms.
  • Preferences honoured in order; when they're full, the engine proposes the closest alternative by distance, price and stars — with a match score.
  • Room-category mixes in one request: 20 Doubles + 10 single-use + 2 Suites.
  • Everything is a draft until your team approves — one click turns the plan into real, ledgered allocations.
End to end

The full programme lifecycle, in one flow.

Hotel contracting→ Pre-allocation by client group→ Client requests→ Allocation round→ Proposal · modifications · acceptance→ AAA signed in-platform→ Phased releases & penalties→ Rooming lists→ Invoices & purchase orders

Release allowances are enforced per property and per phase (e.g. 30% free until 6 months out), with excess charges computed on the average nightly rate of the released rooms — shown to the client before they confirm. Hotel payments follow cumulative phases on the live inventory value, so releases and contract changes are absorbed automatically.

Security & accountability
