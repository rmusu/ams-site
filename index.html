Why acmOS exists

Major events outgrow every hotel tool.

Hundreds of properties. Thousands of rooms. Dozens of delegations — each with contracts, entitlements, phased release rights and rooming lists that change daily. Generic software counts total rooms; your event lives or dies on the 12th of February. So the real work ends up in spreadsheets — slow, fragile, impossible to audit.

Inventory that keeps the nights

Every room tracked per hotel × type × night. Availability is the worst night of the stay — never an average. One short night blocks a booking before it becomes a check-in problem.

AI-assisted allocation, human-approved

The engine matches every request to properties by your priority ranking, entitlements and group blocks — and every proposal comes with a reason you can read. Nothing is written until your team approves.

Numbers you can defend

Contracts, allocations and releases are append-only ledger entries with a named user. When a delegation asks "who released those rooms?", the answer is a query — not a meeting.

The foundation

A room-night ledger, not a room counter.

  • Per-night everything: contracts, allocations, releases and stop-sells, per room type and hotel.
  • Shared physical pools: sell a Double as single-use at its own rate — same rooms, no double counting.
  • Nightly pricing: shoulder nights and event nights priced differently, computed per stay.
  • One-click reconciliation: every counter rebuilds from the movement history, provably.

Allocation Studio

Weeks of Excel. Now an afternoon of review.

  • Your ranking rules: organisations served in the order you set — never first-come-first-served.
  • Blocks & entitlements respected: each group draws only from its reserved rooms, capped at its entitlement.
  • Best alternative, scored: when preferences are full, the closest property by distance, price and stars — with a match %.
  • Explainable, not a black box: every line says why — which choice, which constraint, which trade-off. Your committee can audit the round.
  • Draft until approved: move any line, save across days, then one click writes it all to the ledger.
End to end

From hotel contract to rooming list.

Contracting→ Pre-allocation by group→ Client requests→ ✦ Allocation round→ Proposal & acceptance→ Agreement signed in-platform→ Phased releases→ Rooming lists→ Invoices & POs

Release allowances are enforced per property and per phase — clients see the exact charge before they confirm, computed on the average rate of the rooms they're giving back. Hotel payments follow cumulative phases on the live inventory value, so releases and contract changes are absorbed automatically at the next deposit.
